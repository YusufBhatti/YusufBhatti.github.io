Yusuf Bhatti

Master of Research Student in Climate and Atmospheric Science
​

I am currently studying a Masters by Research in Climate and Atmospheric Science at the University of Leeds researching stratospheric volcanic aerosol. The research for my MRes focuses on modelling the global dispersion and climatic forcing of volcanic aerosol from a notional eruption of Mount Agung in 2017, with the supervision of Dr Graham Mann. I graduated from Keele University in 2018 with a BSc (Hons) in Geology with Physical Geography, having to produce a geological map and report on a 9km2 area of Lipari (Italy). The geological poster can be downloaded here and the report can be downloaded here. My other research interests involve: volcanology, meteorology, and planetary science. In addition to this, I have studied abroad at the University of Utah (2016) for a semester. I undertook classes in atmospheric science, geology and cartography along with the chance to be able to explore the wilderness of America.

Born in Blackburn, I spent most of my school years at Queen Elizabeth's Grammar School. Often, I enjoy visiting other countries to venture where I have never been and exploring rural and urban areas, particularly mountainous regions. More recently I was a field volcanic research assistant for Dr John Murray, with the Open University. This entailed daily surveys on the summit and surrounding areas of an erupting Mt. Etna, measuring and contributing to a 49-year data log on the gravitational spreading and deformation of the volcano. Furthermore, I have accumulated 70+ days of field experience during my studies.
